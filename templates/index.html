Regional Info

Employment Pie Chart

The pie chart and regional information panel details data on selected Core-Based Statistical Regions (CBSAs) with a population of over 1 million. The panel shows the average walkability index for the CBSA, as well as geographic information such as area of water and land within the region. The pie chart shows the employment breakdown between industries in the specified CBSA.

Walkability Mapping

Upon clicking the button above, you will see two visualizations. One map is divided by states the other one is divided up with the states with the smaller populations on the inner part of the circle, and the states with bigger populations on the outer parts. As is clearly shown there is a strong correlation between the size of a states population and its walkability index with the states with the smaller populations having a lower walkability index while the bigger the states population, the higher the walkability index is.

Walkability Bar Chart

The bar chart above selects Core-Based Statistical Regions (CBSAs) with a population of over 1 million. Hovering over each bar details the CBSA name and average walkability rating. The dataset gives a walkability rating to each block group within a region, so the data shown is an average of all block groups within that CBSA. We can see areas like San Juan, PR have low average walkability, but San Jose and Los Angeles has a higher average rating.
